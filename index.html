How to Get Rid of Writer's Block

Rate of Success 90%
Awesome rating: 100% awesome

Stuck in a creative rut? Can't seem to get the words to flow? It's okay! This is normal for every writer at any level. Here are some tips to deal with creative writing blockage.

  • Patience

  1. Take a break.

    As writers, breaks are necessary to maintain composure and to draw ideas. These breaks might consist of going for a walk, listening to music, taking a nap, or hanging out with friends. Taking to time to spend with yourself and your thoughts may lead to a calmer approach to your writing or a "Eureka moment". Allowing your brain to decompress from the stresses of writing will ultimately help the quality of your writing.

  2. Eat a nutritious meal or snack.

    Nutrition is one of the biggest contributors to brain health and function. Fill your meals with complex carbohydrates, plentry of protein, healthy fats, and fruits and vegetables. Not obtaining enough nutrients throughout the day can make you feel sluggish, tired, and slow (which can immensely impact your creative flow). Fueling yourself with the proper nutrients supports your focus, positive mood, and memory.

    A healthy breakfast can kick start your creative brain flow.

  3. Take a shower and change clothes.

    Taking a shower can help refresh and start anew. Aaron Sorkin, an Oscar-winning screenwriter, says he showers eight times a day! He does this to maintain his creative flow when he is experiencing writer's block. This process has helped him write critically acclaimed films like "The Social Network" and "Steve Jobs". Try it, it could work for you too!

  4. Accept the struggle and keep writing.

    Although writer's block is frustrating--especially when you have a deadline--continuing writing may be more help than giving up. Write what you can, even if it isn't perfect, and then edit your rough draft at a later time.

  5. Talk to friends, family, or peers

    Reconnecting with friends and family can help relieve the stresses and frustrations that writing can have. Having loved ones near for reassurance, support, or just a simple conversation has the ability to improve self-esteem and confidence.
